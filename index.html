Patrol briefing no. 05

Six windows. One guardian. No suspicious business.

Learn how to identify danger, guard the correct window, dodge the owner's shush, and use chicken treats.

1

Spot the danger

Watch for the warning triangle outside. Friendly visitors have a round marker—let them pass.

2

Patrol window by window

Move left or right through all six windows, then bark while the visitor is in view. Accurate guarding builds a combo; random woofs test the owner's patience.

3

Outsmart the “shush”

A shush means the owner is Listening in that room. Stay quiet for 2.5 seconds to recover Patience, relocate for one Sneaky Switch, or hide a bark beneath TV or kettle sound cover.

Fill the drumstick meter by guarding well. A chicken treat restores patience and gives Charlie a brief burst of laser focus.
